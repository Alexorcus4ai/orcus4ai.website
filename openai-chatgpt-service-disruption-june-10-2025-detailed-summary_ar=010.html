Global ChatGPT Outage Hits on June 10, 2025

By Abin cofounder of orcus4ai | News | 11 june 2025

On June 10, 2025, many users worldwide experienced major slowdowns or outages while using ChatGPT and other OpenAI tools. Users received errors like “Too many concurrent requests” or saw blank responses. This article breaks down what happened, when, and how to prepare for such outages in the future.

Timeline of the Outage

The issue began around 12:30 PM Indian Standard Time (IST). OpenAI’s status page soon confirmed “elevated error rates and latency” across multiple services—including ChatGPT, Sora, and the API—starting at 4:00 p.m. IST (Indian Standard Time).

By 4:00 a.m. IST (Indian Standard Time), API services had mostly recovered, while ChatGPT gradually returned to normal. Voice mode and Sora lagged slightly but stabilized by June 11 (IST).

What Caused the Outage?

OpenAI said engineers identified the root cause and rolled out fixes. While no technical deep dive was provided, possible reasons include:

  • Infrastructure overload due to high demand
  • Internal configuration or scaling failure

Importantly, OpenAI confirmed it was not caused by a cyberattack or data breach.

Who Was Affected?

The outage had a global impact, affecting users and developers alike:

  • ChatGPT users: Errors, slow response, or complete inaccessibility (free & Plus users).
  • API developers: Widespread timeouts and service disruptions.
  • Sora users: AI video generation errors and failures.
  • Voice features: Delayed recovery after main services resumed.

Reports spanned across North America, Europe, Asia (including India), and Australia. Social media buzzed with memes, frustration, and support, reflecting global dependence on AI tools.

Conclusion

The June 10 outage revealed the deep reliance users have on OpenAI’s tools. Although the issue was resolved the same day, it’s a reminder that outages can hit any platform. Stay proactive: save your work, build resilient systems, and stay informed during AI service disruptions.
