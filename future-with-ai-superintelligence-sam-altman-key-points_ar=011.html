The Future with AI Superintelligence: Key Points from Sam Altman’s Blog

By Abin cofounder of orcus4ai | News | 12 June 2025

AI Breakthroughs:Systems like GPT-4 are smarter than humans in many tasks, amplifying productivity. Scientists are 2-3 times more productive with AI.

Fast Progress:By 2026, AI may generate new insights; by 2027, robots could handle real-world task

Productivity Surge:In the 2030s, people will create software and art easily, with experts still excelling using AI tools.

Abundant Intelligence:AI and energy will become cheap, driving breakthroughs in science, space, and more. A ChatGPT query uses just 0.34 watt-hours of energy.

Singularity in Motion: AI wonders become routine quickly. AI is already helping build better AI, speeding up progress.

Human Continuity:People will still value family, creativity, and simple joys, even as AI transforms life.

Job Shifts:Some jobs will vanish, but new wealth could fund bold policies. Humans will adapt, finding new purposes.

Challenges:

Alignment:AI must align with humanity’s long-term goals to avoid harm.

Access:Superintelligence must be widely available, not controlled by a few.

OpenAI’s Role:Focused on superintelligence research, aiming for safe, accessible AI.

Bright Future:By 2035, AI could unlock unimaginable advances, but humanity will adapt step-by-step.
